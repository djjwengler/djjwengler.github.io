Yardley, Pennsylvania was founded in 1682 by William Yardley. First named "Prospect Farm" by the Yardleys, it was christened "Yardleyville" in 1828. By the time the Reading Railroad came through the town in 1876 it was officially Yardley.

  • Yardley was a station for the Underground Railroad
  • The Bridge in Yardley Borough is listed on the National Register of Historic Places
  • One of the oldest Quaker burial grounds in the state is located in Yardley

Yardley sits on the Delaware river, only a few miles south of Washington's famous Christmas 1776 crossing. The Delaware canal, a 60-mile long canal running alongside the Delaware river, runs straight through Yardley. From 1832 to 1930 the canal was used to ship goods from Northern Pennsylvania to Philadelphia.
